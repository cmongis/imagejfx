Abstract

ImageJ and Fiji distribution exists since 1995 and has integrated numerous plugins over its lifespan. However, their interface poorly evolved making it difficult to browse through all the functionnalities. Thus, when facing browsing big amount of data, ImageJ and Fiji quickly show limitations. Even if other softwares exists, they often require extended knowledge in computer sciences or specific servers. ImageFX bring a refreshment to ImageJ user interface while keeping most of the functionnalities and plugin compatibility. Thus, ImageJFX proposes a guided way to import, browse, annotate and process big amount of images without requiring specific servers or database engine. The ImageJFX database are exportable and dissociable from the data. The annotation process is automated via rules and the images tagged with unique identifiers, allowing recovering of metadata after name or location change. This set of functionnality also ease data sharing, automatic annotatation and image browsing whithout image duplication or specific installation.
